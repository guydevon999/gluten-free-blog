Why do some people avoid gluten?

People might avoid eating gluten for various reasons, most commonly due to health concerns such as celiac disease, which causes an autoimmune reaction to gluten and can lead to serious digestive and systemic issues. Others might have non-celiac gluten sensitivity, which can result in symptoms like bloating, fatigue, and headaches even without the autoimmune response. Additionally, some individuals choose to avoid gluten as part of a broader trend towards gluten-free diets, which they believe might offer health benefits or contribute to overall wellness. For many, avoiding gluten has become a personal or lifestyle choice aimed at improving their quality of life or addressing specific health concerns.

What gluten is and how to avoid it

Gluten is a group of proteins found primarily in wheat, barley, and rye that gives dough its elasticity and helps it rise. It is commonly present in a variety of foods, including bread, pasta, and baked goods, as well as in some processed foods where it may act as a thickening or stabilizing agent. To avoid gluten, individuals should eliminate these grains and their derivatives from their diet. This involves choosing gluten-free alternatives such as rice, quinoa, and gluten-free oats, and carefully reading food labels to identify hidden sources of gluten. Additionally, many specialty products and restaurants now offer gluten-free options to cater to those with dietary restrictions.

Our ambition

Our goal is to serve as a resource for people who are new to being on a gluten-free diet and are looking for helpful information and recipes. Hopefully some of the recipes and content will be exciting to people who have been gluten-free for years as well.
